What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, provide information to site owners, and enable certain features. This site also uses localStorage, a browser storage mechanism similar to cookies but stored locally and not sent to servers with each request.

Essential vs Non-Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are necessary for the site to function properly. They enable core features like caching content for performance and remembering your consent choice. These cannot be disabled without breaking site functionality.

Non-essential cookies are used for advertising and analytics. These are only set after you give explicit consent via the cookie banner. If you reject non-essential cookies, the site will function normally but you will not see personalized advertisements.

How to Manage Cookies

You can manage cookies in several ways:

  • Cookie banner: On your first visit, you can accept or reject non-essential cookies.
  • Reset preferences: Use the button above to reset your cookie consent and see the banner again.
  • Browser settings: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies via their settings. Note that blocking essential cookies may impair site functionality.
  • Ad opt-out: Visit NAI opt-out, DAA opt-out, or Your Online Choices (EU) to opt out of personalized advertising.
